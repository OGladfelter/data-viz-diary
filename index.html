I went for a run today. I was feeling pretty good about my pace, my distance, all that. Of course, about half way through the run, I bumped into Tom, who was also out for a run. Tom and I run together pretty often, but here’s the thing: he’s way faster and runs far more miles each week than I do. So when we do get together to run, it’s usually basically an easy jog for him and a tough workout for me.

We crossed paths just north east of the pond:

When we passed by each other, he looked like he was running twice as fast as I was! I bet he was going longer than I was too. No matter how fast and how far I run, there’s just always someone going faster or going farther…or both!

Jan 7, 2022

Today I ran for the 7th consecutive day in a row! Since my running (and fitness in general) was so lackluster in 2021, I’ve decided I want to really focus on running more this year. And what better way to start off by getting a run in every single day for the first week? Normally I run around three or four days in a row before taking a rest day, so going for a whole straight straight felt like a huge accomplishment…

…that was until I told my friend about it, and he starting going on about his friend who’s been running every single day for the past five years. Apparently there’s an entire website dedicated to tracking “running streaks.” I checked it out, and instantly felt worse about my little 7-day streak. Some of these people have been running straight for years. And yeah, the runners with the biggest streaks are all pretty old - which makes sense logically - but there are plenty of runners who have already have year-long streaks and they’re way younger than I am. Just look at these streakers:

Source: United States Running Streak Association

So now I feel both unaccomplished and old. Great.
