hello world

Jan 7, 2022

Today I ran for the 7th consecutive day in a row! Since my running (and fitness in general) was so lackluster in 2021, I’ve decided I want to really focus on running more this year. And what better way to start off by getting a run in every single day for the first week? Normally I run around three or four days in a row before taking a rest day, so going for a whole straight straight felt like a huge accomplishment…

…that was until I told my friend about it, and he starting going on about his friend who’s been running every single day for the past five years. Apparently there’s an entire website dedicated to tracking “running streaks.” I checked it out, and instantly felt worse about my little 7-day streak. Some of these people have been running straight for years. And yeah, the runners with the biggest streaks are all pretty old - which makes sense logically - but there are some runners who have already have year-long streaks and they’re way younger than I am. Just look at all these streakers:

Source: United States Running Streak Association

So now I feel both unaccomplished and old. Great.
